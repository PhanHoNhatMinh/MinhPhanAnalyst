Sales Analysis

For this project, I delved into the fascinating realm of sales analysis using data from the sales analysis dataset for the year 2019. The dataset, comprising 12 distinct CSV files, necessitated amalgamation into a singular CSV file to facilitate analysis.

1. Cleaning Data!

- Elimination of NaN entries to ensure accurate analysis.
- Utilization of the .astype() method to ensure proper data typing, a crucial step in maintaining data integrity.

2. Data Augmentation

- Introduction of additional columns through Python functions that employed data splitting and calculations, thereby enhancing the dataset's utility.

3. Exploration of Insights

I sought answers to these pivotal questions:

a. Optimal Sales Month

- Visual analysis, in the form of a bar chart, pinpointed December as the highest sales month, trailed by October and April.
- Furthermore, the exceptional sales in December inversely impacted January sales, indicative of the aftermath of holiday season spending.

b. Top Selling Cities

- A bar chart elucidated that San Francisco (CA), Los Angeles (CA), New York City (NY), and Boston (MA) surpassed the average sales figure. This trend could be attributed to the respective cities' standard of living.

c. Prime Advertisement Display Time

- Examination of the bar chart unveiled that 7 PM (19:00), 12 PM (12:00), and 11 PM (23:00) were the most optimal times for advertising, coinciding with post-work hours and breaks. This strategic approach aimed to maximize customer engagement and potential sales.

d. Frequently Sold Combinations

- The dynamic duo of "iPhone & Lightning Charging Cable" secured the top position among frequently sold combinations
- Following suit were the pairs "Google Phone & USB-C Charging Cable" and "iPhone & Wired Headphones."

e. Best-Selling Products and Rationale

- Electronic components and phone accessories emerged as the highest-grossing product categories.
- This outcome was attributed to the shorter lifespan of components and accessories compared to durable items like phones, laptops, and televisions.
